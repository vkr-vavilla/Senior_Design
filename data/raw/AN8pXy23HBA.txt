For today's software engineering interview, please explain how you secure your APIs. First, I always use secure protocols like HTTPS. With this, all data is encrypted during transit so no third party person can read what information is being sent between clients and my server. Okay, that's a good way to protect customer data. What do you do to control access on the server side? I use OOTH plus Open ID Connect to handle authentication and authorization. I also only give users access to a certain subset of my endpoints and information following the principle of lease privilege where users can only access exactly what they need and nothing more. Okay. And how do you handle APIs you expect to interact with the front end? For my APIs that need to interact with a website or front end, I make sure to handle cores gracefully and only allow list the domains that I expect to make requests to my back end. Never allow all. Do you do anything else to protect your service? I implement rate limiting on both a per API key and per IP level to prevent people from making too many requests to my service and ruining availability for everybody
