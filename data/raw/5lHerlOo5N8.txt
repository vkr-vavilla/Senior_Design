For today's software engineering interview, describe a time where you deployed code that took down a production environment. I was working as a software engineer on a network automation team at Amazon and I was in charge of a suite of microservices that handled about 10 million requests per day. I was tasked with migrating a legacy service from Java 8 to Java 17 to improve maintainability and performance. I upgraded to JDK17 locally, fixed a few issues caused by the differences in encapsulation between JDK8 and JDK 17, then deployed to a test environment where everything ran well. I deployed to a pre-prod environment and let the change bake while people were using it for a week to make sure that everything worked well and there were no edge cases that I had missed with real traffic. After that, I partnered with another engineer for a controlled deployment process. Within 5 minutes of the prod deployment, customers started complaining in Slack that the service was completely down. After confirming the change was related to our recent deployment, I immediately rolled everything back and service was restored. And what did you do after you fixed the issue in production? After service was restored, we worked on finding the root cause, which was a difference in the way that the runtime was set between prod and pre-pro environments. I wrote a detailed post-war document and created alarms and test cases to make sure this never happens again in the future.
